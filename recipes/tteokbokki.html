Tteokbokki

Description

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food made from chewy rice cakes (tteok) cooked in a spicy, sweet, and savory red chili sauce called gochujang. It often includes fish cakes, boiled eggs, and scallions, and is sometimes garnished with sesame seeds and green onions. Tteokbokki is known for its rich flavor and comforting texture, making it a favorite snack or meal among Koreans and food enthusiasts worldwide.
  1. Prepare Ingredients: Soak 1 pound of cylindrical rice cakes (tteok) in water for about 10 minutes if they are hard. Slice 2-3 fish cakes and chop 2 green onions.
  2. Make Sauce: In a pot, mix 2 cups of water, 3 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean red chili paste), 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes), 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and 1 tablespoon of minced garlic.
  3. Cook Ingredients: Bring the sauce mixture to a boil over medium heat. Add the soaked rice cakes and fish cakes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. Simmer: Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the rice cakes are soft and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Add Garnishes and Serve: Add chopped green onions and optional hard-boiled eggs, stir gently, and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Garnish with sesame seeds if desired and serve hot.
